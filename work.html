Dhaka Peak-Hour Traffic Variance Study

Quantitative Research on Urban Mobility

  • Objective: To investigate how commute durations across five major office corridors in Dhaka fluctuate during peak/off-peak hours to build a data-driven business case for flexible corporate schedules.
  • Approach: Extracted 150 proxy observations via Google Maps and employed Two-factor ANOVA with replication to test route and time slot effects on travel time.
  • Key Insights: Discovered a statistically significant interaction effect (p < .001) between route and time, disproving the assumption of uniform city-wide commute times.
  • Recommendation: Advised implementing staggered, route-specific flexible working hours rather than a one-size-fits-all policy to effectively minimize commute fatigue.
  • Tools: Microsoft Excel (Data Modeling & ANOVA), Google Maps, Quantitative Research Methods.

Two Decades of Bangladeshi TVCs: An IMC Perspective

Academic Research & Content Analysis

  • Objective: To trace the strategic evolution of television commercials across the Telco, FMCG, and BFSI sectors in Bangladesh from 2000 to 2026, evaluating how cultural and macroeconomic shifts influenced creative messaging.
  • Approach: Conducted a qualitative content analysis of landmark TVCs utilizing models like DAGMAR and the Hierarchy of Effects. My core contributions centered on historical FMCG sector research, alongside the technical compilation and formatting of the final manuscript.
  • Key Insights: Mapped a definitive industry shift from early functional product claims to deep, culturally rooted emotional storytelling, and ultimately to purpose-led omnichannel integration.
  • Recommendation: Critiqued ongoing industry challenges such as celebrity endorsement saturation and copycat concepts, advising brands to invest in original, locally resonant narratives and stricter ethical self-regulation.
  • Tools: Qualitative Research, IMC Frameworks, Academic Content Analysis.
