Dhaka Peak-Hour Traffic Variance Study

Quantitative Research on Urban Mobility

  • Objective: To investigate how commute durations across five major office corridors in Dhaka fluctuate during peak/off-peak hours to build a data-driven business case for flexible corporate schedules.
  • Approach: Extracted 150 proxy observations via Google Maps and employed Two-factor ANOVA with replication to test route and time slot effects on travel time.
  • Key Insights: Discovered a statistically significant interaction effect (p < .001) between route and time, disproving the assumption of uniform city-wide commute times.
  • Recommendation: Advised implementing staggered, route-specific flexible working hours rather than a one-size-fits-all policy to effectively minimize commute fatigue.
  • Tools: Microsoft Excel (Data Modeling & ANOVA), Google Maps, Quantitative Research Methods.
